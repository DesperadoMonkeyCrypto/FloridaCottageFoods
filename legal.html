Florida Cottage Food Law — Plain-English Guide (2025)

Summary of sales caps, where you can sell, labeling, allergens, and shipping rules. This page is educational; always check current statute and UF/IFAS guidance.

At a glance

  • Sales cap: up to $250,000/year (track your records).
  • Where you can sell: direct to consumers, including online and by mail delivery within Florida. No wholesale to retailers.
  • Labeling: name/address, product name, ingredients by weight, net weight/volume, allergens, and the statement: “Made in a cottage food operation that is not subject to Florida’s food safety regulations.”
  • Examples allowed: baked goods (non-PHF), candies, jams/jellies with proper pH, dry mixes, etc.

Starter checklist

  1. Pick products that qualify (non-potentially-hazardous).
  2. Set labels with required elements (see above).
  3. Decide service area (pickup, delivery, or ship within FL).
  4. Create a simple order flow (DM/website/phone) and keep receipts.
  5. Add your listing to the statewide directory: Florida Cottage Foods.
